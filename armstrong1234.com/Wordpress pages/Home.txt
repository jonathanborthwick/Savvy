<h1>Our History</h1>
[/vc_column_text][vc_empty_space height="36px"][vc_column_text]When America entered WW1, Hal, too young to go to war, made a promise to his older brother Jack. Hal promised Jack that when he returned from ‘<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B6hRDS3LvQQ" target="_blank">over there</a>’ his property maintenance business would still be here.

That here was New York City and the year was 1917. When Jack returned in 1919, he found that Hal had not only been true to his word, but also that his younger brother had a knack for the business. Going forward together, the two brothers would build a company that would become one of the largest and most successful building maintenance companies in the State.

In 1954, Hal's daughter Patricia married Howard. Howard at the time was an apprentice in Pittsburgh, PA for a local property improvement company. Hal, having worked with and managed hundreds of carpenters and journeymen, could tell right away there was something special about this young man.  Sensing that under Howard's leadership there was an opportunity to create a building maintenance company setting the highest standard in the industry, Hal made the decision to back Howard. The year was 1957 and the company was  Armstrong of Pennsylvania.

In 1966, after a decade of operating successfully, Howard made the decision to move Armstrong to California. Looking back over the last 50 years, we recognize Howard's legacy and the company he built.  Armstrong has performed tens of thousands of jobs in every size imaginable, including both residential and commercial properties. Armstrong's history over the last half century is entwined with the history of Northern California. Many have heard our testimonials, which include some of the most notable personalities of the <a href="http://www.armstrong1234.com/armstrong-san-francisco/">San Francisco Bay Area</a>. Baseball great Willie McCovey, Hall of Famer Jim Plunkett, and 49ers Ronnie Lott and Roger Craig. Armstrong painted Bing Crosby's mansion and has been featured on This Old House...

But no matter how many houses and buildings we improve, Armstrong continually researches and reviews the ongoing performance of our materials, as well as the installation methodologies of our installers.
